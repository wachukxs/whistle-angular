"Whistleblowing is one of the most effective ways to detect and prevent corruption and other malpractice. Whistleblowers' disclosures have exposed wrongdoing and fraud, helped save millions in public funds, avoid disasters for health, the environment." – Transparency.org

Remember Nigeria's 5% Recovered Loot Policy?[1] LuxLeaks? Panama Papers?

"Unfortunately, reporting often comes at a high price: whistleblowers risk their career, their livelihood and sometimes their personal safety to expose wrongdoing that threatens the public interest. They may be fired, sued, blacklisted, arrested, threatened or, in extreme cases, assaulted or killed. And in some societies, whistleblowing carries connotations of betrayal rather than being seen as a benefit to the public" – Transparency.org

Sometimes, they get a fraction the payout.

"Ultimately, societies, institutions and citizens lose out when there is no one willing to cry foul in the face of corruption" – Transparency.org

But if people are able to 1. call out corruption anonymously, 2. And/Or receive whatever associated payout anonymously via crypto... then we can put up a good fight against the various forms of corruption.
